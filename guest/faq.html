Frequently Asked Questions

What is UUID?

The Universally Unique Identifier (UUID) is a 16-octet (128-bit) number, which is "practically unique", but not "guaranteed unique".

What does UUID Registry do?

UUID Resgistry allows you to generate and register an UUID. Thus it will be guaranteed unique in the subset of registered UUIDs.
